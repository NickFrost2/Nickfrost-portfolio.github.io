FAQs

I specialize in a variety of graphic design services, including:
  • Branding: Logo design, visual identity, brand style guides.
  • Print Design: Business cards, brochures, posters, packaging design.
  • Digital Design: Website design, email templates, social media graphics, digital ads.
  • UI/UX Design: User interface design and user experience for web and mobile apps.
  • Illustration: Custom illustrations for various purposes, such as editorial and marketing.
  • Motion Graphics: Animation for web, social media, and video content.

The timeline for a project depends on its complexity and scope. Typically:
  • Logo design: 1–2 weeks.
  • Website design: 3–4 weeks.
  • Branding packages: 4–6 weeks.
However, I will always provide a customized timeline during our initial consultation, keeping you informed of progress along the way.

My design process typically follows these steps:
  1. Discovery: We discuss your goals, preferences, and expectations. I may ask you to fill out a design questionnaire.
  2. Research: I research your industry, competitors, and trends to ensure your design stands out.
  3. Concept Creation: I create initial design concepts based on your brief and feedback.
  4. Revisions: After presenting the concepts, we refine them together through feedback and iterations.
  5. Final Delivery: Once you're satisfied with the design, I prepare and deliver all necessary files in various formats (e.g., .ai, .png, .jpg, .pdf).

Yes, I offer revisions as part of my design process. I typically include two rounds of revisions with the initial design concept to ensure it aligns with your vision. Additional revisions may be subject to a fee, depending on the project and the cause of issue.

The cost of a project depends on several factors such as:
  1. Scope: The number of designs, complexity, and deliverables (e.g., logo, branding kit, website).
  2. Time-frame: How quickly you need the project completed.
  3. Revisions: The number of revisions and feedback rounds required. I provide custom quotes for each project after discussing your needs and goals.
